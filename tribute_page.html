Did you know?

Sir Christopher Lee has played 6 different roles with the title of "Count". The most famous ones are Count Dracula (Dracula) and Count Dooku (Star Wars).

Timeline

  • 1922 - Born in Belgravia, London
  • 1926 - Moved to Swizterland with mother after parents' divorce
  • 1928 - Played as Rupelstilktskin in Miss Fisher's Academy
  • 1929 - Returned to London
  • 1931 - Entered Summer Fields School, a prep school in Oxford
  • mid 1930's - Entered Wellington College
  • 1939 - Dropped out of Wellington due to step-father's bankrupcy
  • 1939 - Spent summer vacation in France, Worked for United States Lines, a mail company in London, and enrolled in military academy
  • 1941 - Joined the Home Guard, then the Royal Air Force, eventually joining RAF Intelligence
  • 1946 - Entered the entertainment industry by joining Two Cities Films
  • 1947 - Debuted in Corridor of Mirrors
  • 1952 - Got his breakthrough by appearing in several movies by British National Studios
  • 1958 - Played Count Dracula for the first time in Dracula
  • 1962 - Played Sherlock Holmes for the first time in Sherlock Holmes and the Deadly Necklace
  • 1971 - Played as Jekyll and Hide in I, Monster
  • 1973 - Played Dracula for the last time in The Satanic Rites of Dracula, Comte de Rochefort in The Three Musketeers and Lord Summerisle in The Wicker Man
  • 1974 - Played Francisco Scaramanga in The Man With the Golden Gun, a James Bond film
  • 1977 - Moved to America
  • 1991 - Played Sherlock Holmes for the last time in Sherlock Holmes and the Leading Lady
  • 2001 - Played Saruman for the first time in The Lord of the Rings
  • 2002 - Played Count Dooku in Star Wars: Attack of the Clones
  • 2005 - Played Dr. Wilbur Wonka in Charlie and the Chocolate Factory
  • 2009 - Knighted for his services to drama and charity
  • 2011 - Played Saruman for the last time in The Hobbit
  • 2015 - Died in London at age 93

“Making films has never just been a job to me, it is my life. I have some interests outside of acting – I sing and I’ve written books, for instance – but acting is what keeps me going, it’s what I do, it gives life purpose.”

-- Sir Christopher Lee
